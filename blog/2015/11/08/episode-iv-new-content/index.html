Episode IV - New Content

Nov 8th, 2015

It is finally here, I have added new material to my site! This time around I have created a new page to house information about my scientific publications and presentations. This includes links to my published research articles and copies of my past conference poster presentations, all of which are now freely available.

I had looked into publishing my past poster via the figshare platform, but this seemed overkill for some very old posters which have undergone publication as research articles since being presented. Therefore these previous presentations are being hosted via public folders on my dropbox account. In the future, I would be keen to publish my posters via figshare. I see this as a fantastic way to promote research to a wider audience, especailly as those who were able to attend sessions to see posters can be quite few in number. From my own experience, I would have liked to have seen a few posters from this years Society for Neuroscience meeting, which I was unable to attend this year. My boss did however present our latest work, but as this was published in Journal of Neuroscience the exact same week ( Moore et al. (2015) JNeurosci) it seems more appropriate to promote the article rather than the poster. I am hoping to go to FENS next year so perhaps that would be a good opportunity to try out figshare.

At the moment my PhD thesis available online, this is due to the submission predating the requirement for an additional electronic submission at the University of Surrey. I very much doubt it would get any attention, but I am a believer in free and open research so it is definitely something that I shall be looking into. Perhaps the best way would be to ask the library at the university to add it to those that they are currently hosting. Alternatively I could host it via dropbox public folder which is my current method for my posters, but it would be great to have a more appropriate and prestigious home, plus a link from the British Library would be nice.
